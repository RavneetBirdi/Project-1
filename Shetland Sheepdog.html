Shetland Sheepdog

 

size: Shetland Sheepdogs are tiny to medium-sized dogs. The average shoulder height of a male sheepdog is 13 to 16 inches (33-40 cm), whereas that of a female sheepdog is 13 to 15 inches (33-38 cm).

Weight: Typically, they weigh 15.8-25.3 pounds (6.8-11.3 kg).

 

Coat: Shelties have two coats: a long, straight, rough outer coat and a soft, dense undercoat. They need to be groomed frequently to avoid matting and shed seasonally.

Colour: They are available in a variety of hues, such as bi-blue (grey and white), tricolour (black, white, and tan), and sable (a mixture of brown and black).

 

Eyes: Their almond-shaped, dark brown eyes exude alertness and intelligence.

Ears: Shetland ears Sheepdogs appear alert and lively due to their short, triangular ears that stand straight.

Tail: Long and feathered, their tail is often carried low when they are at ease and higher when they are alert or agitated.

Body: They have an athletic, well-proportioned body with a deep chest and a level back.

Trainability: They have a strong desire to please and adapt well to training techniques that rely on positive reinforcement.

Energy Level: To maintain their happiness and well-being, Shetland Sheepdogs need frequent exercise and mental stimulation due to their high level of activity.

Alertness: They are great watchdogs because of their reputation for loud, pointed barking.
