Border Collie

 

Size: Border Collies are medium-sized dogs; females are slightly smaller at 18 to 21 inches (46 to 53 cm), while males usually stand between 19 and 22 inches (48 to 56 cm) height at the shoulder. Typically, they weigh between 14 and 25 kg (or 30 to 55 pounds).

Coat: Their double coat is thick. The medium-length outer coat might have a smooth or rough texture. There is a thick and soft undercoat. There are many other colour combinations for border collies, such as black and white, tricolour, red and white, red and tan, or merle.


Eyes: Usually brown but occasionally blue, the breed's eyes are one of its most defining characteristics.

 

Ears: The medium-sized ears of Border Collies are perched atop their heads. They frequently stand up when they are alert, although they can also be entirely lowered or partially erect.

Tail: When a dog is calm and relaxed, its medium-length tail is carried low; but, when the dog is aroused or vigilant, it may be lifted and coiled gently.


Body: Border Collies thrive at a variety of canine sports and activities thanks to their muscular and nimble bodies. Their body is well-proportioned, including a straight back and a deep chest.

Gait: Border Collies are renowned for having a smooth, agile gait that covers ground. Their effortless, light, and springy movements are frequently praised, which is very useful for herding sheep.

Expression: Due to their highly developed mental skills, Border Collies frequently exhibit an attentive and intelligent expression. They are renowned for having keen intelligence and rapid learning.
