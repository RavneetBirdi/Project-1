Australian Cattle Dog

 

Physical characteristics: ACDs have a robust, muscular frame and are medium-sized canines.
Known as "blue" or "red" heelers, their coat is short and dense, frequently displaying a mottled or speckled pattern.
The breed is renowned for its sharp gaze and attentive features.
They have two coats: a rough, weather-resistant outer coat and a soft undercoat.


Colouration: As previously indicated, ACDs might have a coat that is speckled, red, or blue in colour.
The most prevalent colour is blue, which can range in tone from light blue to dark blue and is frequently speckled with tan, white, or black.
Red ACDs have a reddish colour and may have tan or white markings on them.
Red and blue tones combine to give speckled ACDs a unique mottled loo

Body Structure: They are great working dogs because of their powerful legs and well-developed chest.
The breed usually has a large head, a muzzle that is fairly pointed, and a skull that is slightly curved.

Intelligence: Australian Cattle Dogs are incredibly smart and able to pick up difficult skills.
They are frequently utilized in dog sports including agility, obedience, and herding competitions since they are excellent obedience trainers.


Temperament: ACDs are noted for their steadfast work ethic, loyalty, and protective behaviour.
They can be reticent or distant and are cautious around new people.
Due to its high energy level, the breed needs mental and physical stimulation on a regular basis.


Herding Instincts: Strong herding tendencies are exhibited by ACDs, as seen by their propensity to snap at the heels of other animals.
