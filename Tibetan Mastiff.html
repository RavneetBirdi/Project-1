Tibetan Mastiff

Dimensions and Build: Tibetan Mastiffs have massive bones and are powerful, huge canines. Their long necks and robust, muscular builds contribute to their intimidating appearance. They have the ability to grow to a shoulder height of 24 to 26 inches (61-66 cm) and weigh up to 70 to 150 pounds (32-68 kg).

Coat: The Tibetan Mastiff is bifurcated in its coat. The undercoat is dense and soft, while the outside coat is thick, coarse, and straight or slightly wavy. The dog's coat aids in shielding it from the chilly climate of the Tibetan Plateau, where it originated. The coat might have tan markings or not, and it can be any colour:- black, brown, blue, gold, or red.

Head and Face: Tibetan Mastiffs have a powerful, square-shaped muzzle and a massive, heavy head with a broad skull. They resemble lions due to their characteristic mane of hair around their necks. The deep dark eyes of this breed, which are usually almond-shaped and spaced widely apart, are its most defining attribute.

Tail and Ears: The Tibetan Mastiff has medium-sized, triangular ears that are perched above the skull. When the dog is vigilant, they are carried forward; depending on the dog's temperament, they may drop totally or remain semi-erect. Typically, the dog carries its long, bushy tail coiled over its back, especially when it is in motion.

Temperament: The autonomous, guarding, and territorial attitude of Tibetan Mastiffs is well-known. They may be distant or reserved with strangers, yet they are faithful and loyal to their relatives. In order to guarantee that they develop into well-rounded and well-behaved companions, early socialization and training are crucial.

Lifespan: A Tibetan Mastiff can live up to 14 years on average, but some can survive up to 15 years with the right care.

Maintenance: Tibetan Mastiffs don't need a lot of maintenance, even with their long, thick coat. All that's needed to maintain the health of their coat is the occasional bath and regular brushing. They do, however, shed significantly twice a year, and more frequent brushing is required to control the shedding during this time.

Exercise: Even though Tibetan Mastiffs don't have a lot of energy, they still need to get regular exercise to stay happy and healthy. They need to play in a safe environment and go for daily walks to ensure their wellbeing.

Health: Hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, and thyroid disorders are among the health concerns that Tibetan Mastiffs, like many dog breeds, are susceptible to. In order to lower the chance of passing these and other genetic disorders on to their progeny, conscientious breeders will check their dogs for them.
